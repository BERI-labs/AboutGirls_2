# Ashford College — Knowledge Base

---

## Admissions

Entry points at Year 7 (age 11), Year 9 (age 13), and Sixth Form (age 16).

**Year 7 entry:** The Ashford Entrance Exam is held each January. The exam covers English, mathematics, and verbal reasoning. Shortlisted candidates are invited to interview in February. Places are confirmed by the end of February, with acceptance required within two weeks.

**Year 9 entry:** Candidates sit the ISEB Common Pre-Test in Year 6 or 7, followed by a school-specific assessment in English and mathematics. Interviews are held in the Lent term.

**Sixth Form entry:** A minimum of 6 GCSEs at grade 7 or above is required. Subject-specific interviews are held in November for the following September's intake. Conditional offers are made based on predicted GCSE grades.

Registration deadline: 31 October for all entry points. A non-refundable registration fee of £150 applies.

Open mornings are held in October and November each year. Private tours are available year-round by appointment through the Admissions Office.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/admissions/guide-2024

## Tuition Fees

Years 7–11: £7,200 per term (£21,600 per annum). Sixth Form (Years 12–13): £7,800 per term (£23,400 per annum).

Fees are reviewed each September; the current schedule is effective from September 2024. Lunch is included in the termly fee for all year groups.

Extras not included in fees: residential trips, individual music tuition (charged per term), and external examination entry fees.

Payment is due by the first day of each term. The school accepts direct debit (monthly or termly) and termly lump sum payments. A 2% discount is offered for annual payment in advance.

Sibling discount: a 5% reduction on the younger sibling's fees applies when two or more siblings attend concurrently.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/fees/schedule-2024

## Scholarships

Academic scholarships are available at 11+, 13+, and 16+ entry points, offering up to 20% fee remission. Awards are made on the basis of entrance exam performance and interview.

Music scholarships offer up to 15% fee remission. An audition is required; instruments considered include piano, strings, voice, and brass. Candidates should normally have reached Grade 5 or above on their principal instrument.

Sport scholarships offer up to 10% fee remission. A trial is required; sports considered include rugby, swimming, and athletics. Candidates should demonstrate county-level or equivalent ability.

Means-tested bursaries are available up to 100% of fees for families with demonstrable financial need. Bursary applications are assessed independently and confidentially by the Bursar's office.

Scholarship applications close two weeks before the relevant entrance assessment. All awards are reviewed annually; holders must maintain academic and conduct standards to retain their award.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/fees/scholarships

## Co-curricular

Over 80 clubs and societies run weekly across lunch and after school, covering academic, creative, and recreational interests.

The Duke of Edinburgh's Award is offered at Bronze (Year 9), Silver (Year 10), and Gold (Year 12). Ashford College has a 95% completion rate at Gold level.

Programmes include: Model United Nations, debating society, chess club, coding club, film society, drama society, Young Enterprise, and Amnesty International group.

The annual whole-school production takes place each autumn term. The inter-house drama competition is held in the spring term. Both events are staged in the 350-seat Ashford Theatre.

The trips programme includes language exchanges with partner schools in France, Germany, and Spain. Year 9 pupils undertake an expedition week in the Lake District. A biennial World Challenge expedition takes Sixth Form students to destinations in South-East Asia or South America.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/cocurricular/prospectus

## Music

The Music Department runs three orchestras (Symphony Orchestra, Chamber Orchestra, and String Ensemble), two concert bands, and four choirs including a chamber choir and gospel ensemble.

Individual and small-group tuition is available in over 25 instruments, taught by visiting specialist teachers. Lessons are arranged on a rotating timetable to minimise disruption to academic classes.

The annual Spring Concert is held at Elmbridge Civic Hall and is the flagship event of the music calendar. Additional concerts include the Christmas Carol Service, Lunchtime Recital Series, and House Music Competition.

The purpose-built music school houses a recording studio, twelve practice rooms, and an industry-standard music technology suite used for A-Level Music Technology.

ABRSM and Trinity Guildhall examination preparation is offered to all instrumental pupils. The school regularly achieves Distinction rates above 70% in external music examinations.

A chamber music programme for advanced players runs throughout the year, with regular masterclasses hosted by visiting professional musicians from leading London ensembles.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/music/handbook

## Sport

Core sports: rugby, football, cricket, athletics, swimming, netball, tennis, and cross-country. All pupils participate in timetabled games sessions twice per week.

The high-performance programme supports county and national-level athletes, with links to Surrey County Cricket Club, Harlequins Rugby Academy, and Swim England South East regional programme.

Facilities include: a modern sports hall, 25-metre six-lane indoor swimming pool, fully equipped gymnasium, fitness suite, and 14 acres of playing fields with dedicated rugby, football, and cricket pitches.

Recent honours: Surrey U15 Rugby Champions 2024; National Schools Swimming Championships finalist 2024; Surrey Girls Netball Cup winners 2024; ESSA Team Swimming relay gold 2024.

Partnerships with Elmbridge FC, Surrey Cricket Board, and Swim England South East provide additional coaching, fixture opportunities, and talent identification pathways for pupils.

**Source:** https://www.ashfordcollege.ac.uk/sport/season-review-2024

## Welcome to Haberdashers' Girls' School

Haberdashers' Girls' School (Habs Girls) is a leading independent day school for girls aged 4 to 18, located on a 100-acre co-educational campus in Elstree, Hertfordshire. Sharing a site with Haberdashers' Boys' School, Habs Girls offers the "best of both worlds": a single-sex education tailored to how girls learn best, coupled with extensive opportunities for co-educational collaboration and socialisation.

Our purpose at Habs is to empower every student to make a profound impact in the world. We are an academically ambitious and inspirational school that values the importance of developing the whole child to create a genuine sense of belonging. Led by Headmistress Dr Hazel Bagworth-Mann, our commitment is deeply rooted in enabling our young people to succeed academically while developing the independence, resilience, and strong moral compass required to create positive change.

We foster a culture of scholarship and intellectual curiosity. This curiosity ensures students do not just excel in examinations but develop an enduring love of learning. By enabling students to achieve their full potential, find their passions, grow as individuals, and invest in our community, we ensure they leave us ready to make their mark on society.

Habs Girls and Habs Boys share founding principles of philanthropy and education, a 100-acre site on the outskirts of north London, a reputation for excellence both inside and beyond the classroom, and a shared vision, mission, and strategy for the future. The school motto is **"Together, boundless."**

## History

### Founded in 1875

In 1868, the Schools Inquiry Commission examined the state of girls' education in Britain and found only 12 endowed schools for girls compared with 820 for boys. This led to the creation of the Hospital at Hoxton of the Foundation of Robert Aske. A girls' school was established alongside the boys' school in Hoxton, both officially opened by HRH Prince Arthur of Connaught on 5 June 1875.

### Late 1800s

By 1893, both schools found their Hoxton location unsuitable. Examination results had improved, but the number of enrolled girls was falling. In 1898, a purchase was agreed on the Springfield Estate in Acton.

### Early 1900s

The new school in Acton was officially opened on 1 July 1901 by Mrs W M Tudor, wife of the Master of the Worshipful Company of Haberdashers. Designed for 300 students, it was soon outgrown. In 1910, a new extension almost doubled the capacity to 500 students. In 1926, a sports field was purchased near North Ealing station. In 1932, the school opened an outdoor swimming pool. In 1935, the full school uniform was introduced.

### World War II

On 3 September 1939, the girls' school was evacuated, with 216 students and 11 staff taking the train to Dorset. The school returned and reopened in September 1940. On the night of 12 October 1940, a direct hit damaged the library and dining room. Teaching continued throughout the remainder of the war.

### 1950s to 1970

The war-damaged buildings were rebuilt in the early 1950s. Student numbers increased to 650 by 1948 and 720 by 1958. A new Lower School building was added in 1961. Surrounded by private homes, the school had no room to expand and looked to relocate a second time.

### 1970 to 1980

In 1972, a girls' school began to be built on a site adjacent to the boys' school at Elstree. The new premises were opened by HRH Princess Margaret in October 1974. The indoor swimming pool opened in 1976. The same year, the school became independent following the demise of the direct grant scheme. The Sime Music School opened in 1979.

### 1980 to 2020

By 1991, the Sixth Form had doubled. Rainbow (Reception) was introduced in 1994, and student numbers rose from 750 to over 1,100. By 2005, with 1,140 students, Habs Girls had become one of the largest independent girls' schools in the country. In 2005, the St Catherine Singers won BBC Radio 3 Youth Choir of the Year. School houses were introduced in 2015, named after the first six headmistresses — Powell, Millar, Gilliland, Sprules, Harold, and Gillett. The STEM building was completed in 2019.

### 2020 to Present

In 2019, the schools embarked on a new strategy with closer collaboration between the two schools, bringing boys and girls together in A-Level teaching sets, through the Habs Diploma, and in academic and co-curricular activities.

In 2021, the community responded to the discovery that Robert Aske had invested part of his estate in the Royal African Company, which was involved in the slave trade. Robert Aske's name was dropped and the motto was changed to "Together, boundless."

In 2023, Dr Hazel Bagworth-Mann arrived as the new Headmistress.

## Vision, Values & Ethos

The school's vision is to be at the forefront of education. Its mission is two leading schools on one co-educational campus for highly ambitious and curious students, offering an outstanding education that is fun, exciting, and intellectually challenging, developing independence, resilience, and a moral compass to create positive change. Its purpose is to empower young people to make a profound impact in the world.

Life at Habs Girls is described as an adventure, a thrilling opportunity for discovery and progress. Students are encouraged to create lasting positive change throughout their lives, for the benefit of themselves, society, and the environment. No two Habs students are the same, but their values and behaviours set them apart: they are ambitious, globally curious, tolerant and resilient, with a desire to improve themselves and the world. A Habs student is compassionate, kind and altruistic, with a strong moral compass.

The four core values underpinning the school are:

- **Ambition:** Bold and ambitious — for themselves, for others, and for the world. Underpinned by altruistic and philanthropic foundations. We nurture leaders who aim to influence society positively and inspire those around them.
- **Curiosity:** Creating and using every opportunity to engage with the world; asking questions, challenging assumptions, and expressing ideas. We cultivate a deep intellectual curiosity, encouraging students to ask difficult questions, explore beyond the syllabus, and engage in magical, wondrous learning.
- **Courage:** Bold in approach to learning and life. Not afraid to take risks, make mistakes, and learn from them. We teach our students to be courageous in their learning and their convictions, embracing challenges and understanding that resilience is key to growth.
- **Community:** Looking out for one another and committed to making a positive difference. Celebrating diversity and fostering an inclusive environment where everyone belongs. Kindness and worthwhile actions are central to our shared success.

## Pastoral Support & Wellbeing

Pastoral care is the heartbeat of Habs Girls. The school places strong emphasis on proactive and responsive pastoral care, ensuring students feel safe, secure, happy, and known as individuals. Students are encouraged to build warm and positive relationships with all members of the Habs community, creating a strong sense of belonging where kindness and consideration for others is central.

### Peer Mentoring: Amigas and Buddies

- **Amigas (Senior School):** Older students are trained to act as 'Amigas' to younger students (particularly incoming Year 7s), offering a friendly face, peer guidance, and support as they navigate Senior School life.
- **Buddies (Junior School):** A similar system operates in the Junior School, ensuring that new joiners and younger girls always have an older peer to turn to for help and friendship.

### PSHE and Medical Care

Our Personal, Social, Health and Economic (PSHE) education is proactive and responsive to the needs of modern young women. If friendship issues occur, the school is noted for dealing with them directly and introducing relevant topics into the curriculum. The on-site Medical Centre is staffed by qualified professionals to support both the physical and mental wellbeing of all students.

### Mobile Phone Policy

The school maintains strict guidelines on mobile phone usage to ensure a focused, safe, and socially engaging environment during the school day, prioritising face-to-face interaction and mental wellbeing.

## Diversity and Inclusion

Habs is proud of the huge range of cultures, backgrounds, identities and beliefs represented in its community. This is not only welcoming for students but forms an invaluable preparation for life and work in a diverse society and globalised world. The school has a dedicated Head of Diversity and Inclusion who directs the approach to student admissions and welfare, curriculum-building, staff recruitment, and teacher training.

**Celebrating everyone:** The school celebrates the uniqueness and contribution of every student, across all races, genders, gender identities, sexual orientations, religious beliefs, cultures, social backgrounds, and disabilities. Students are encouraged to explore their own interests, develop independent thought, and express ideas sensitively and effectively — building empathy, courage, and resilience.

**Active bystanders:** All students and staff are encouraged to be effective 'active bystanders' — aware of someone else's inappropriate or threatening behaviour and empowered to challenge it.

**Faith at Habs:** Students come from a wide range of faith backgrounds, as well as families where no faith is practised. Regular assemblies are held for Buddhist, Christian, Hindu, Jain, Jewish, Muslim, and Sikh communities. Spirituality is a regular dimension in celebrations and class discussions. Students are encouraged to explore, challenge, and confirm their own spirituality.

**Practical aspects of inclusion:**
- Lunch options include vegetarian, non-beef, halal, and kosher. The school respects and accommodates students who fast for faith reasons.
- The school hosts events around Christmas, Hannukah, Eid, and Diwali. Whole-school assemblies include a 'carousel' of prayers, and a multi-faith room is available for all students.
- An annual Pride Party is a festival-like celebration open to all students and staff.
- Students can join identity and cultural societies including African and Caribbean Society, Christian Union, Muslim Society, J-Soc, South Asian Society, and LGBTQ+ Society.

**Disabilities and conditions:** The school makes reasonable adjustments to support students with physical disabilities, sensory impairments, and learning or cognitive conditions (including autism, dyslexia, attention deficit, dyscalculia, and aphasia). Exam accommodations include modified papers, extra time, a reader or assistant, bilingual dictionaries, or assistive technology.

**Bursaries:** The school believes financial circumstances should not prevent the brightest students from attending, and offers a generous bursary programme.

## VAT Treatment

VAT at 20% applies to: fees, devices, Junior School lunch (included in fees), and educational/instructional clubs. VAT exempt: music lessons (provided via external peripatetic teachers at the Girls' School), home-to-school coach, educational trips, childcare/welfare-only clubs, and exam fees. Fees In Advance paid after 29 July 2024 are subject to VAT. Fees In Advance paid before 29 July 2024 may be exempt but are under HMRC scrutiny.

The Board of Governors decided in September 2024 to absorb some of the impact of VAT, meaning the net termly increase (inclusive of VAT) was 14% rather than the full 20%.

VAT is charged on scholarships only on the amount paid by parents (e.g. a 10% scholarship means VAT applies to the 90% paid by parents).

## Habs Diploma

Taken alongside A-Levels by all Sixth Form students, the Habs Diploma provides an important pre-university experience to support students for life at university and beyond. It helps embed intellectual curiosity, an enduring love of learning, and the wide range of skills needed to flourish in an ever-changing world. It also builds a deep understanding of other perspectives, backgrounds, and cultures.

The Diploma focuses on developing: knowledge and scholarship, independence, leadership, verbal self-expression, critical thinking and problem-solving, technological literacy, creativity, global-mindedness, cultural and social awareness, and analytical thinking.

**Components of the Habs Diploma:**

- **Extended Project Qualification (EPQ) or Extended Research Project (ERP):** Students choose between these options to conduct in-depth independent research beyond the exam curriculum. The EPQ is a Level 3 qualification equivalent to half an A-Level, externally marked, carrying UCAS points, and well recognised by universities. Students can view this year's and previous years' ERPs on the school website.
- **Visiting Speaker Programme:** A varied mix of keynote speakers and discussions arranged through academic societies.
- **Elective Courses:** Designed to equip students with modern-world skills and cultural capital, including beginner languages, coding, finance, business, leadership, theatre appreciation, and more. Students taking three A-Levels take electives; those taking four A-Levels do not take additional electives.
- **Subject Enhancement:** Students delve deeper into one of their chosen A-Level subjects or an area of study they may consider at university, supported by expert subject staff.
- **Community Service:** A choice of activities to help students make a profound impact in the world.

**Source:** https://www.habselstree.org.uk/girls/sixth-form/inside-the-classroom/habs-diploma/

## Drama

At Habs, the creative arts are recognised as vital: for telling stories, developing emotional intelligence, fuelling imagination, and collaborating to create work that can affect others. The school takes a fresh, innovative approach to theatre-making, moving away from traditional school plays and instead focusing on creatively ambitious work that puts the process of making at its heart.

Students are encouraged to be bold — to take risks, be in the moment, and use their own experiences and imaginations. The school aims for work to be socially conscious, reflecting, celebrating, or challenging the world around us.

**Productions and programme:** At least three large-scale productions are staged each year, alongside participation in the Coram Shakespeare Schools Festival, the National Theatre New Views playwriting programme, and numerous House Drama events. Plays range from adaptations of books, graphic novels and classic texts to new work devised by the company or created alongside professional writers. Recent productions include *Brief Encounter*, *This Is Somewhere Better*, *Made of Empty Spaces* and *How Can You Learn to Swim Before You Get In?* — many jointly with Habs Boys.

**Professional collaborations:** Students work with industry professionals including playwrights James Fritz, Simon Longman, Ella Frears and Andrew Muir; theatre-makers Matt Harrison, Joyce Henderson and Marc Benga; movement directors Kane Husbands, Rachel-Leah Hosker and Grace Gibson; and film companies Tea Films and 4Ransom Films. The school has also produced a full-length feature film, *Tiny Acts of Revolution*, with Tea Films.

**Film:** Habs is developing students as film-makers, with training from professionals in scriptwriting, directing, acting for screen, camera operation, and cinematography.

**National opportunities:** Over the past eight years, 35 students from Habs Girls and Habs Boys have been accepted into the National Youth Theatre of Great Britain. Several Habs students have had professional readings at the National Theatre; Jamila Salim's play *Colour Outside the Lines* was bought by Audible for professional production.

**Technical roles:** Students can take on lighting, sound, AV, stage management, set design, costume, make-up and video design roles — trained by in-house Drama Technicians.

**Arts Award:** Drama activities contribute towards the Arts Award, a national qualification at Explore, Bronze, Silver and Gold levels. The Gold level is worth 16 UCAS points.

**Performance spaces:** A drama studio, a purpose-built black-box studio for teaching and examination shows, and a designated performance space with tiered auditorium seating and high-quality lighting and sound equipment.

## Sport

Sport at Habs Girls is led by Acting Co-Directors of Sport, Lauren Scott and Lizzie Wright. The PE and sport provision is a vibrant, inclusive programme with high levels of student engagement across all year groups. The recent ISI report highlighted that the breadth, depth, and quality of the sports provision is exceptional.

Sport at Habs is much more than competition and results — it is an integral part of the school experience, promoting resilience, teamwork, and leadership in every student. The school's sporting philosophy is underpinned by **"The Habs Way"**: being well-presented and articulate, fiercely competitive but always respectful, showing confidence without arrogance, striving for excellence, and maintaining a zero-tolerance approach to bullying. Students, coaches, and spectators are all expected to model these behaviours.

The sports programme supports every individual, whether representing the school at county, regional, or national levels, or simply enjoying the social and wellbeing benefits of physical activity. The programme spans over 40 sports, with students competing at district, county, and national level. The school runs a busy sports calendar accessible via SOCS. With up to six teams per age group in weekly competitive action, opportunities to compete at every level are extensive.

**Three big themes:** Active Wellbeing (encouraging lifelong healthy habits and physical literacy from Reception to Upper Sixth); High Performance (creating an environment for students and teams to compete at the highest levels); and Character and Values (developing skills so students leave as better people, anchored in the school's core values).

**PE and Games curriculum:** All girls in Years 7, 8, and 9 receive one period of PE per week, covering Swimming (including water polo), Gymnastics, Dance, Health Related Fitness, Tennis, and Cricket. In addition, all students participate in one afternoon of Games (Lacrosse or Netball in Autumn/Spring terms; Athletics in Summer). Sports Science is available as a two-year GCSE from Year 10, and as an A-Level in the Sixth Form (co-taught with Habs Boys).

**Collaboration with Habs Boys:** Girls regularly play in boys' cricket and football teams; there are combined teams for athletics, squash, gymnastics, and swimming. The annual Joint Sports Day, held at the StoneX Stadium, brings together all students from Year 7 and above from both schools under combined house banners. The HabsDash — a weekly 3.5km inclusive community run every Tuesday — welcomes around 170 runners each week, with themed events including the SantaDash, PurpleDash (for Papyrus Charity), GivingDash, and RainbowDash. A record 650 runners participated in the annual GivingDash.

**Sports partnerships:** The school has strong links with professional organisations including London Pulse, Tottenham Hotspur FC, Middlesex County Cricket, and Watford Water Polo, offering students professional-level training and competition.

### High Performance Programme (HPP) and Sports Scholarships

Each year the Habs Sport team selects a number of students who show exceptional promise and commitment for inclusion in the High Performance Programme. Students are placed in either the **Gold** or **Silver** HPP, based on their current level of sporting performance, potential for further development, and expectation to act as positive role models. Sports Scholars are automatically placed in the Gold programme. The Directors of Sport have the final decision on selections. The HPP is available to students from Years 7 to Upper Sixth.

**Sports scholarships** are awarded in recognition of sustained sporting excellence, talent and future potential. Candidates should be active members of top-ranked school sports teams and involved in sporting clubs outside school with representative experience at county level or above. Candidates are encouraged to demonstrate potential in at least one of the focus sports at Habs: netball, lacrosse, and athletics.

**All enrolled HPP students receive:**
- Access to a strong regional and national fixture list
- Partnership relationships with elite clubs, academies, and development pathways
- Outstanding coaching from the best coaches within Habs or local professionals
- Access to some of the very best sports facilities in the UK
- Access to the full range of co-curricular activities
- Regular high-quality sports tours and visits within the UK and abroad
- Expectation to act as ambassadors for sport at Habs and model The Habs Way

**HPP Gold Package (for Sports Scholars and highest-level athletes):**
- Individualised mentoring sessions with a member of the Habs Sports team
- Support in managing workload and balancing high-performance sport with school life
- Individualised strength and conditioning programme with Habs Athletic Development staff, coordinating with clubs/academies
- Regular fitness assessments and monitoring
- Nutrition programme delivered by an external provider (Holroyd Howe)
- Video analysis of individual technical aspects of play or team performance
- Off-site visits including lab testing and career pathway exploration
- Enrolment on the Independent Coach Education webinar series 'Supporting Aspiring Athletes Programme'

**HPP Silver Package:**
- Mentoring sessions with a current Sixth Form student at Habs Girls, at least once a term
- Individualised strength and conditioning programme with Habs Athletic Development staff, coordinating with clubs/academies
- Regular fitness assessments and monitoring
- Video analysis of individual technical aspects of play or team performance
- Off-site visits including lab testing and career pathway exploration
- Access to seminars and workshops run by specialists

## The Habs Innovation Centre

The Centre for Innovation realises Habs' 2030 vision to be at the forefront of education. Being at the forefront of education does not mean simply doing well at delivering the ordinary — it means striving to be extraordinary. A dedicated Director of Innovation was appointed in April 2024 to support this vision across both schools.

The Habs curriculum is ambitious and innovative, but the school believes being at the forefront of education means students serve more than their own interests. Creative and critical thinking, problem-solving, deep understanding, and effective collaboration will be essential to success not just in work but in life. Big data, greater reliance on technology, and the development of generative AI prompt a rethinking of what and how students need to be learning.

**The Centre's work has five strands:**

1. **Establishing the Habs Centre for Innovation** — a test-bed classroom with observation options, CPD events, a pedagogy resource library, and teacher training sessions.
2. **Digital Innovation** — exploring AI and its role in education, including how to use it responsibly. Habs has published its thinking on AI in education, including a book launch event on the topic.
3. **Curricular Innovation** — exploring areas where Habs can make a real difference, including Global Changemakers (EDI, Sustainability, and Entrepreneurship) and Tech and Ethics (AI and civic responsibility).
4. **Habs Dispositions Project** — mapping and presenting the school's profound impact and developing the role of its values.
5. **Imagining the Future of Teaching** — research into pedagogy for a rapidly changing world.

Recent STEM achievements include a Year 9 team triumphing at the VEX Robotics Regionals, showcasing strength in computer science and engineering.

**Source:** https://www.habselstree.org.uk/girls/senior/inside-the-classroom/the-habs-innovation-centre/
